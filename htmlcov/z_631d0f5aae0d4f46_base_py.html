117 def coupling(self, scatterer: BaseScatterer) -> Quantity: 

118 r""" 

119 Compute the light coupling between the detector and a scatterer. 

120 

121 The coupling quantifies the interaction between the field captured by the detector and the scattered field produced by the scatterer. Mathematically, the coupling is calculated as: 

122 

123 .. math:: 

124 |\iint_{\Omega} \Phi_{det} \, \Psi_{scat}^* \, d \Omega|^2 

125 

126 Where: 

127 

128 - \( \Phi_{det} \): The capturing field of the detector, representing the sensitivity of the detector to the incoming scattered field. 

129 - \( \Psi_{scat} \): The scattered field produced by the scatterer. 

130 - \( \Omega \): The solid angle over which the integration is performed, typically covering the full \( 4\pi \) steradians around the scatterer. 

131 - \( d\Omega \): The differential solid angle element. 

132 

133 This integral computes the overlap between the detector's sensitivity pattern and the scattered field, which is then squared to represent the power coupled into the detector. 

134 

135 Parameters 

136 ---------- 

137 scatterer : BaseScatterer 

138 The scatterer object that interacts with the incident light, producing the scattered field. 

139 

140 Returns 

141 ------- 

142 Quantity 

143 The power coupling between the detector and the scatterer, expressed in watts (W). This value represents the amount of scattered power that is captured by the detector. 

144 

145 Notes 

146 ----- 

147 - The method internally invokes the appropriate binding method based on the type of scatterer (e.g., Sphere, Cylinder) to calculate the coupling. 

148 - The coupling depends on both the geometry of the detector and the nature of the scattered field, making it essential for evaluating the efficiency of light collection in scattering experiments. 

149 

150 Example 

151 ------- 

152 A common use case is to evaluate how much of the scattered light from a nanoparticle is captured by a photodiode or integrating sphere. The result can be used to estimate the efficiency of light collection for scattering measurements. 

153 

154 """ 

155 return getattr(self.binding, "Coupling" + type(scatterer).__name__)(scatterer.binding) * watt

290 def get_poynting_vector(self, scatterer: BaseScatterer, distance: Quantity = 1 * meter) -> float: 

291 r""" 

292 Compute the Poynting vector norm, representing the energy flux density of the electromagnetic field. 

293 

294 The Poynting vector describes the directional energy transfer per unit area for an electromagnetic wave. It is defined as: 

295 

296 .. math:: 

297 \vec{S} = \epsilon_0 c^2 \, \vec{E} \times \vec{B} 

298 

299 Where: 

300 

301 - \( \vec{S} \): Poynting vector (W/m²) 

302 - \( \epsilon_0 \): Permittivity of free space (F/m) 

303 - \( c \): Speed of light in vacuum (m/s) 

304 - \( \vec{E} \): Electric field vector (V/m) 

305 - \( \vec{B} \): Magnetic field vector (T) 

306 

307 The cross product of the electric and magnetic field vectors results in the Poynting vector, which represents the flow of electromagnetic energy in space. 

308 

309 Parameters 

310 ---------- 

311 scatterer : BaseScatterer 

312 The scatterer object that interacts with the incident electromagnetic wave, affecting the fields and energy flow. 

313 distance : Quantity 

314 The distance at which the Poynting vector is computed. 

315 

316 Returns 

317 ------- 

318 Quantity 

319 The norm of the Poynting vector, which gives the magnitude of the energy flux density in watts per square meter (W/m²). 

320 

321 Notes 

322 ----- 

323 The Poynting vector is computed over a 3D mesh of voxels that cover the entire solid angle of \( 4\pi \) steradians. This method calculates the local energy flux at each voxel and returns the norm, which represents the magnitude of energy flow at each point in space around the scatterer. 

324 

325 The Poynting vector is fundamental in understanding how energy is transmitted through space in the form of electromagnetic waves. 

326 

327 Example 

328 ------- 

329 This method is used to assess the distribution of energy around a scatterer. The total energy flow can be obtained by integrating the Poynting vector over the surface enclosing the scatterer. 

330 

331 """ 

332 Ephi, Etheta = scatterer.get_farfields_array( 

333 phi=self.binding.mesh.phi, 

334 theta=self.binding.mesh.theta, 

335 r=distance 

336 ) 

337 

338 E_norm = numpy.sqrt(numpy.abs(Ephi)**2 + numpy.abs(Etheta)**2) * volt / meter 

339 

340 B_norm = (E_norm / c).to('tesla') 

341 

342 poynting = epsilon0 * c**2 * E_norm * B_norm 

343 

344 return poynting.to(watt/meter ** 2)

346 def get_energy_flow(self, scatterer: BaseScatterer, distance: Quantity = 1 * meter) -> Quantity: 

347 r""" 

348 Calculate the total energy flow (or radiated power) from the scatterer based on the Poynting vector. 

349 

350 The energy flow is computed using the following relationship between the scattered energy and the incident intensity: 

351 

352 .. math:: 

353 W_a &= \sigma_{sca} \cdot I_{inc} \\[10pt] 

354 P &= \int_{A} I \, dA \\[10pt] 

355 I &= \frac{c n \epsilon_0}{2} \, |E|^2 

356 

357 Where: 

358 

359 - \( W_a \): Energy flow (W) 

360 - \( \sigma_{sca} \): Scattering cross section (m²) 

361 - \( I_{inc} \): Incident intensity (W/m²) 

362 - \( P \): Radiated power (W) 

363 - \( I \): Energy density (W/m²) 

364 - \( c \): Speed of light in vacuum (m/s) 

365 - \( n \): Refractive index of the surrounding medium 

366 - \( \epsilon_0 \): Permittivity of free space (F/m) 

367 - \( E \): Electric field (V/m) 

368 

369 The total power is computed by integrating the intensity over the surface area of the scatterer. 

370 

371 Parameters 

372 ---------- 

373 scatterer : BaseScatterer 

374 The scatterer object, which contains information about the scattering properties of the particle, such as geometry and material. 

375 distance : Quantity 

376 The distance at which the Poynting vector is computed. It should change the computed total power. 

377 

378 Returns 

379 ------- 

380 Quantity 

381 The total energy flow (radiated power) from the scatterer, expressed in watts. 

382 

383 Notes 

384 ----- 

385 This method computes the energy flow by calculating the Poynting vector (which represents the directional energy flux) and summing it over the surface mesh of the scatterer. The final result is the total radiated power. 

386 

387 """ 

388 poynting_vector = self.get_poynting_vector(scatterer=scatterer, distance=distance) 

389 

390 dA = self.binding.mesh.d_omega * distance ** 2 

391 

392 total_power = 0.5 * numpy.trapz(y=poynting_vector, dx=dA) 

393 

394 return total_power
